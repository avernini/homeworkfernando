Step 3: Discussion & Analysis (15 min)

Honesty vs Social Norms

Analyze the spectrum of responses when declining invites. There are two major routes: being "brutally honest" or giving a "lame excuse". Let's investigate both models.

Refusal Type A

Lame Excuses 🤥

A minor white lie, weak explanation, or convoluted story designed to protect the requester's feelings, though it lacks genuine substance.

"I agreed to help a friend move and I don't know what time we're going to be done."
Refusal Type B

Brutal Honesty 🔪

Direct, unfiltered sincerity with zero polite padding. Simply stating that you lack the willingness or interest to join.

"I just would rather not, and will not, be attending your party."

BONUS Slang & Idiomatic Guide

To be down to

To be ready, willing, or highly eager to perform or participate in an activity.

"I'm definitely down to check out that new rooftop setup."
Count me in

To explicitly request that someone includes you inside their plans or activities.

"If you guys are getting street food tonight, count me in!"
Turn down

To reject or decline an offer, proposal, invitation, or application.

"It is hard for me to turn down a weekend beach party."
Count on me

To assure someone they can completely rely or depend on you for support or presence.

"If you need someone to help you move, you can count on me."
