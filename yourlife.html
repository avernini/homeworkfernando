Click on the vocabulary pills below to verify their technical applications. Click the icon to trigger premium native speech parameters:

distracted /dɪˈstræk.tɪd/

Unable to concentrate or pay full attention because your mind is captured by alternative events.

"Users are easily distracted by infinite feeds, sharing data without thinking."
figure out /ˈfɪɡ.jɚ aʊt/

To finally understand, discover, or solve a complex problem or logic puzzle after systematic analysis.

"We need to figure out how algorithms track our location settings in background."
immortality /ˌɪm.ɔːrˈtæl.ə.t̬i/

The ability to remain alive, active, or stored forever without degradation. In tech, it represents unerasable digital trails.

"Our data achieves absolute immortality once it passes to cloud servers."
threaten /ˈθret.ən/

To declare an intention to cause damage, leakage, or structural problems to a person if specific rules are not obeyed.

"Ransomware hackers often threaten to release private company datasets."
turn out /tɝːn aʊt/

To develop in a specific way or have a surprising, unpredicted final result.

"It may turn out that the app was quietly capturing background analytics all along."
Step 2: Grammar Mapping (10-12 min)

Modals of Advice, Obligation & Digital Hygiene

Securing your digital footprint requires utilizing appropriate modal verbs of strength to deliver exact instructions and rules.

The Obligation Spectrum
Modal Strength Level Digital Governance Application Case
must / mustn't 99% — Rigid Prohibitions / Absolute Rules "You mustn't reuse legacy passwords on secure banking networks."
have to / don't have to 80% — Regulatory Need (External Source) "Companies have to encrypt all transactional databases by law."
should / shouldn't 55% — Recommended Advice / Best Practices "You should think twice before posting unverified images."
Syntax Loss Protection
Avoid Illegal Auxiliary Doubling

You mustn't to post credentials on forums.

Correct Way

You mustn't post credentials on forums. (Modals always skip 'to')
