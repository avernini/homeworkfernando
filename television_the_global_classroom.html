Step 2: Vocabulary (10-12 min)

Industry Lexicon & Definitions

Map the exact television terms to your vocabulary bank. Click on any pill to preview its CEFR-aligned definitions and correct contextual usage:

to binge watch verb

To watch multiple episodes of a TV series in rapid succession, typically in a single sitting.

"I had the best weekend ever! I binge watched the entire first, second, and third seasons of Twin Peaks."
telly, gogglebox, idiot box nouns

Popular slang terms for the television set. "Telly" is highly common in British English, whereas "idiot box" emphasizes mindless watching.

"There's nothing but absolute rubbish on the telly these days."
soap (opera) noun

A serial drama show, typically broadcast daily, focusing on the melodramatic, interconnected lives of a recurring cast of characters.

"Emmerdale is a soap that follows the lives of various families living in a small village in Yorkshire."
sitcom noun

Short for "situational comedy." A humorous TV show revolving around a fixed cast of characters navigating everyday environments (like a home or workplace).

"Cheers, set in a fictional bar of the same name, is a highly popular sitcom following a group of regulars."
episode, season, series nouns

An individual program (episode); a collection of episodes released in a specific year or period (season in US/UK English); the entire run of the show (series).

"Did you see the latest episode of Game of Thrones? I cannot believe how little Jon Snow actually knows!"
spinoff noun

A new TV show or movie derived from an existing, successful piece of intellectual property, usually focusing on a popular supporting character.

"The Mandalorian is a spinoff from the Star Wars films that follows a lone bounty hunter."

Step 4: Grammar Focus (12-15 min)

The Generational Media Pivot: habits vs. experiences

To jump from A2 to B2, you must clearly distinguish between what you used to do (habits from your past childhood) and what you have done (your experiences up to now). Analyze the contrast below:

📜

Past Habits (Used to)

Use used to + verb for things you did frequently in the past, but don't do anymore. Ideal for talking about your childhood TV habits.

➔ "I used to watch cartoons every Saturday morning when I was ten."

(Now I don't do this anymore. It is a closed past habit.)

Formulation:

Subject + used to + base verb

🎯

Life Experiences (Present Perfect)

Use have/has + past participle for life achievements or things you have done up to this point in your life. The exact time doesn't matter!

➔ "I have watched the entire series of Breaking Bad three times."

(My experience up to today. I don't specify the exact date.)

Formulation:

Subject + have/has + past participle

Fossilized Syntax Guard (A2-B2)
Avoid Mixing Simple Past with Present Perfect Timestamps

I have watched a sitcom last night.

If you name a specific past time ("last night", "yesterday"), you must use Simple Past.

Correct Way

I watched a sitcom last night. / I have already watched that sitcom. (Simple Past vs. Undefined Experience)

Step 5: Output & Boardroom (15 min)

The Broadcasting Monopoly: Streaming vs. Cable

Let's discuss the societal impacts, monetization shifts, and psychological side effects of television in the 21st century. Activate these complex debate prompts with your trainer using your new B2 grammar anchors:

Audit 1: Streaming Platforms

"Are streaming platforms (Netflix, HBO Max, Prime) superior to traditional cable television sets? Is it better to have a variety of streaming services or would it be better if all TV shows were on just one platform?"

💡 B2 Pivot Challenge: Try to use used to to compare how you watched TV in your childhood vs. how you do it today.
Audit 2: Societal & Children Impact

"Do children watch too much TV these days? What effect does TV have on kids? Do parents use the TV like a babysitter? Is there too much violence and sexual content on TV these days?"

💡 B2 Pivot Challenge: Use Present Perfect to talk about trends that have occurred over the last ten years (e.g., *"Streaming platforms have changed how..."*).
Audit 3: Stereotypes & Geopolitical Influences

"What kind of stereotypes are common to see on TV programmes? How does TV influence people's lives and how did it change the world?"

💡 B2 Pivot Challenge: Express how TV characters and stories used to be very stereotypical compared to now.
